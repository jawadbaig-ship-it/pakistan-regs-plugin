# Anti-Money Laundering Act, 2010

**Regulator:** GoP / FMU  
**Reference:** AML Act 2010  
**Date:** 2010 (amended)  
**Applicable to:** All Reporting Entities (Banks, DFIs, MFBs, NBFCs, EMIs, exchange companies, real estate, jewellers, accountants, lawyers, etc.)  
**Source:** [https://www.fmu.gov.pk/docs/AMLA-2010.pdf](https://www.fmu.gov.pk/docs/AMLA-2010.pdf)

## Purpose & Scope

Primary AML/CFT statute - establishes the National Executive Committee, General Committee, and Financial Monitoring Unit (FMU); criminalises money laundering and predicate offences.

## Key Sections / Requirements

- Definitions - money laundering, predicate offence, property, suspicious transaction
- Establishment of FMU and its powers
- Reporting Entity (RE) obligations - CDD, STR, CTR, record keeping
- Powers of investigation and prosecution
- Freezing, seizure and confiscation of property
- National Risk Assessment (NRA) framework
- Designated Non-Financial Businesses & Professions (DNFBPs)
- Penalties, offences and protection for reporting in good faith
