# Banking Companies Ordinance (BCO), 1962

**Regulator:** GoP  
**Reference:** Banking Companies Ordinance LVII of 1962  
**Date:** 1962 (amended)  
**Applicable to:** All Banking Companies (commercial, Islamic, foreign, DFIs, MFBs)  
**Source:** [https://www.sbp.org.pk/l_frame/BCO_1962.pdf](https://www.sbp.org.pk/l_frame/BCO_1962.pdf)

## Purpose & Scope

Primary banking law - governs licensing, business scope, prudential and conduct rules for banking companies in Pakistan. Most SBP regulatory powers derive from BCO.

## Key Sections / Requirements

- Licensing of banking companies and prohibitions
- Permissible forms of business (Section 7)
- Reserve Fund and dividend restrictions
- Cash reserves and SLR
- Section 25-A - confidentiality of credit information (eCIB basis)
- Section 41 - bank inspection by SBP
- Section 41-B - directives by SBP
- Restrictions on loans to directors/related parties
- Banking Mohtasib Pakistan provisions
- Penal provisions and prosecutions
